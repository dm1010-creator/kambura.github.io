Part One

The Name Is the Argument

A Geographic Indicator (GI) is a name on a product that tells you exactly where it came from. Not just "Kenya" — that is too big. Not just "Embu" — still too big. The name of the specific place: Kavutiri.

When a name points to a real place with genuinely special soil, altitude, and farming knowledge, it becomes a guarantee of quality. Buyers pay more. Farmers get paid more. The community builds something nobody can copy — because nobody else has that land.

Without a GI, Kavutiri QISHR looks the same on a shelf as product from anywhere in Kenya. The farmer gets a commodity price. With a GI, the Kavutiri name is the product.

This GI is not owned by one person. It belongs to every farmer on this land. Registration with KIPI (Kenya Industrial Property Institute) makes the name legally protected for the whole community.

The Soil Under Kavutiri

Nitisols are the finest agricultural soils in East Africa. Deep (often over 1 metre to bedrock), well-drained, structurally stable because their clay minerals bind into shiny-faced "peds."

Coffee roots go deep — accessing moisture and minerals through dry seasons. The volcanic parent rock (IB2 — intermediate basalt) releases iron, calcium, and magnesium slowly, building flavour complexity in the cherry.

Altitude 1,400–1,600 m slows cherry development, concentrating sugars and acids in the fruit wall — the part that becomes QISHR husk.

Scarcity 3.70× — this Nitisol type is nearly four times rarer across Kenya than the national average. Kavutiri's soil is not ordinary. The label has the right to say so.

Part Three

The Evening Ritual

6:00 PM — Prepare

Fill a clean 1-litre flask with freshly boiled water. Add 4 heaped tablespoons of Gibbs QISHR with a thumb of fresh ginger, half a cinnamon stick, and two cardamom pods. Cover. Leave it alone. Let time do the work.

7:30 PM — Convene

Pour. Share. Listen. There is no perfect cup. There is only the cup prepared with care.

Anytime — Brew it cold

Add 6 tablespoons to a bottle of cold water. Refrigerate 12 hours. A golden amber drink that needs nothing else.

Part Five

The Label — Every Element Earns Its Place

  • QISHR
    Arabic قشر — husk, skin. Names 600 years of coffee cherry infusion history. Indexes the whole lineage without requiring explanation.
  • KAVUTIRI · Kirinyaga · Kenya
    The GI claim. Identifies the specific origin: Nitisol KE162, volcanic basalt IB2, 1,500 m altitude, Kirinyaga County. No other place can use this name.
  • Cherry cross-section illustration
    Shows all five layers — skin, pulp, parchment, silver skin, bean — with "dried husk" annotated. Teaches the buyer what they are drinking before they open the bag.
  • Ginger · Cinnamon · Cardamom
    Transparency on ingredients and flavour expectation. The qishr spice tradition — unchanged since 15th-century Yemen.
  • Ethiopia · Yemen · Kenya flags
    The provenance arc: Ethiopia (wild plant origin), Yemen (cultivation and naming tradition), Kenya (current terroir). 600 years in three flags.
  • Coffea arabica L. · Net wt. 250 g
    Scientific precision and weight statement — legal requirements for sale in Kenya and for export. Both are also trust signals to serious buyers.
Part Six — The Steps

How the GI Gets Built

01
Form the group

At minimum 3 farmers in Kavutiri agree to use the name together and to maintain a common quality standard. The GI belongs to all of them equally.

02
Document the place

GPS: −0.420250, 37.499712. Soil polygon KE162. The KML boundary file already exists. Photograph the farm, drying beds, the husks. These go with the KIPI application.

03
File with KIPI

Kenya Industrial Property Institute, Kabarsiran Avenue, Nairobi. Filing fee: KES 5,000. The positioning document downloadable from this page is the draft of your application.

04
Get the KEBS mark

Kenya Bureau of Standards certifies quality once the GI is registered. The KEBS mark on the label tells buyers in Kenya and Europe that the standard is real and verified.

05
Approach buyers

Specialty roasters in Nairobi. German and UK importers already sourcing cascara. The EU Novel Food approval (EFSA 2022) means the European market is legally open. Kavutiri gives buyers the origin story they need.
