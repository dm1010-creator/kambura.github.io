The premise

A garden built to undo what a season indoors does to you.

Rustic Garden sits on the fertile slopes near Mount Kenya, where a single-source coffee farm, a chlorine-free swimming pool, and a working aquaculture project share the same few acres with stands of trees planted for no reason other than the fact that people need them nearby.

Everything below is drawn from what actually happens on that land — not a wellness brochure, but the four things worth bringing to a place that currently has neither trees nor coffee in easy reach.

Where it's grown

Sourced by hand, roasted with restraint.

Every cherry is hand-picked at its peak by organic farmers on the slopes near Kavutiri, then honey-processed — a method that trades speed for flavor, letting fruit sugars settle into the bean during sun drying.

Light roasts keep the citrus and floral notes intact; dark roasts lean into smoke, chocolate, and a smooth, low-acid finish. A French press draws out the widest range of flavor across both — a moka pot suits a stronger, darker cup.

Also growing on site

Beyond the four

Drip irrigation, done properly

Water delivered straight to the roots — less evaporation, deeper roots, and fewer nutrients lost to runoff than a hose ever manages.

An arrowroot variety in progress

The garden is currently breeding its own Maranta arundinacea arrowroot, alongside a network of local farmers trained in composting.

Macadamia & mango, in the works

Early-stage value addition on macadamia and mango — worth asking about directly if you're sourcing beyond coffee.

Vegetative propagation

The garden breeds its own seed stock, favoring vegetative propagation for reliability over generations.
